Common Motifs and Symbols

Alcohol Abuse

Alcohol Abuse is something that is seen a lot around the world, a way to drown sorrows when things go wrong, when there is a loss that shatters everything, when the responsibilities become too much to handle. Sometimes, the alcohol is to forget the past, forget what one has gone through, forget everything that has happened, but going to Alcoholics Anonymous meetings help provide that comfort to get to the root of the problem. Jacquie Red Feather goes to AA meetings because she could never quite stay sober as long as she hoped, drinking became her crutch that she couldn’t let go of, but she wanted to be able to go back to her family finally. Harvey was an AA meeting leader, and he met Jacquie again at the AA meeting. It wasn’t just Jacquie, but Dene Oxendene as well as Jacquie Red Feather Tony Loneman that dealt with alcohol abuse in other ways. Tony’s mother drank while she was pregnant with him; meanwhile, Dene’s uncle drank himself to death.
