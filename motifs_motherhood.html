Common Motifs and Symbols

Motherhood

Mothers play an important role in There There. Often, we also see that the mothers are single or remarried. Often the grandmothers also play an important role in the lives of children and even young adult characters. This could represent and mean many different things for the book. For instance, this could be reminiscent of the matrilineal societies that many Native peoples belonged to. It can also be a kind homage to the strength of women and their role in keeping families together and children alive in times--sometimes through generations--of great hardships. Mothers, though they are all different, play important roles in all the lives of the main characters.

Characters and Their Relationships with Motherhood

    [LINK ALL CHARACTERS TO PAGE]
  • Tony Loneman was raised by his grandmother, Maxine.

  • Dene Oxendene’s mother is single.

  • Opal Viola is the grand aunt of Orvil, Lony, and Loother. She raises the boys. Her mother was also often single.

  • Edwin Black’s mother is a white woman who is currently dating Bill Davis.

  • Jacquie Red Feather was a single mother to Jamie, the mother of Orvil, Lony, and Loother. As the sister of Opal, she also had a mother who was often single.

  • Orvil’s mother, Jamie, passed away.

  • Blue is the daughter of Jacquie Red Feather, whom she gave up for adoption.
